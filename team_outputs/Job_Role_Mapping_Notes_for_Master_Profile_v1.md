## 3. Role-by-Role Framing Instructions

### 3A. When Applying to PROGRAMME DIRECTOR Roles

**Framing emphasis:**
- Lead with discovery → mobilisation → delivery → operational handover lifecycle
- Foreground RAID, programme board, steering committee, governance structure language
- Use milestone, dependency, risk, and scope change vocabulary
- Emphasise programme scale: concurrent workstreams, multi-vendor, cross-functional, cross-border
- Quote programme value or infrastructure scope (£3M network build; 1,200 endpoints; 7 data centres)

**Role-to-evidence mapping:**

| Russell's Role | What to Foreground |
|---|---|
| LVS Bulgaria (2024–2025) | Greenfield programme mobilisation; discovery to live ops; multi-vendor orchestration; first-of-kind for LVS Europe |
| Sitehands (2018–2020) | Multi-workstream infrastructure transformation; 7 DC migrations; 25,000 Wi-Fi endpoints; M&A-driven delivery |
| CentricsIT (2020–2024) | 15+ concurrent programmes; governance framework; executive reporting to C-suite |
| Atos (2025–present) | NHS live estate programme; zero-disruption delivery; EUC + network modernisation |
| Collective IP (2025–present) | Technical programme management; concurrent engagement |

**Language to use:**
- "Mobilised and led a complex multi-workstream programme…"
- "Established programme governance, RAID, and steering committee reporting…"
- "Delivered against agreed scope, budget, and timeline with zero-disruption constraints…"
- "Led discovery, sequencing, and risk assessment to mobilise programme from standing start…"
- "First-of-kind [X] deployment — greenfield from inception to live operations…"

**Language to avoid for PD roles:**
- "Managed operations" (too BAU)
- "Contract-to-cash" (ops/commercial language; not needed for PD)
- "Cost-to-serve" (same)

- Use team leadership evidence (24 engineers at LVS; dispersed EMEA teams at CentricsIT)

**Role-to-evidence mapping:**

**Language to use:**
- "Held full P&L accountability across a multi-programme, multi-country operation…"
- "Owned end-to-end commercial lifecycle: procurement, SLA governance, billing and revenue assurance…"

The "de facto" qualifier at CentricsIT should be dropped for director-level applications. The accountability was director-level; the title does not matter. Frame as: *"Operations & Infrastructure Director, EMEA (CentricsIT / Park Place Technologies) — 2020–2024."*

### 3C. When Applying to OPERATIONS MANAGER Roles

**Only pursue if:**
- The day rate is compelling (£450+/day for contract)
- The employer is a growth/scale-up where "Manager" understates the real scope
- The role is a stepping stone to a named OD/PD role within the same client
- The role brief describes director-level accountability with a manager title (common in SMEs and PE-backed businesses)

**Framing emphasis:**
- Compress strategic signals; foreground operational execution
- Emphasise team leadership at delivery level (not board level)
- Use ITIL-adjacent language even if not formally certified
- Focus on Atos and Comunica as primary evidence roles

**Language to use:**
- "Managed day-to-day delivery operations across multiple live sites…"
- "Led engineering teams through change windows, site readiness, and operational transitions…"
- "Ensured SLA compliance and service continuity across a live, mission-critical estate…"
