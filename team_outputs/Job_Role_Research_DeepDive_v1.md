The Operations Director in a UK technology or infrastructure context is accountable for the end-to-end operational performance of a technology business unit, managed service, or infrastructure estate. - Maintain operational risk, compliance posture and audit-readiness
- Represent operations at board/executive level; produce KPI packs and executive reporting

## Part 3: Where Roles Overlap vs. Truly Differ

### Genuine Overlaps
- All three require **senior stakeholder management** (board/executive reporting at Director level; client-facing ops reporting at Manager level)
- All three require **vendor and third-party governance**
- All three need **commercial awareness** (though depth and authority differ)
- All three operate in **multi-vendor, multi-site UK tech environments**
- Risk management language appears in all three â€” though RAID-level formal governance is Programme Director territory

### True Differentiators

**Operations Director vs. Programme Director:**
- Ops Director = permanent operational accountability (always-on); Programme Director = fixed-term transformation mandate
- Ops Director owns people, culture, and BAU; Programme Director owns scope, milestones, and change
- Ops Director is typically permanent or long-term interim; Programme Director can be shorter-term contract
- P&L is core to Ops Director; budget-within-programme is the Programme Director's commercial territory

**Operations Director vs. Operations Manager:**
- Director = board-facing strategy and P&L; Manager = execution and team
- Director sets the operating model; Manager implements it
- Director holds vendor relationships at contract level; Manager coordinates day-to-day
- Career progression: Manager â†’ Director (typically 3â€“5 years' gap in seniority)

**Programme Director vs. Operations Manager:**
- Minimal genuine overlap â€” these are distinct career tracks (delivery leadership vs. service operations)
- Programme Director: temporary, strategic, change-focused; Operations Manager: permanent, tactical, BAU-focused
- The only overlap is in complex MSP environments where project delivery touches ongoing service operations
