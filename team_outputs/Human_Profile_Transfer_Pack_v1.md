## HOW TO USE THIS PACK

This pack translates the master profile into three target role lanes. For each lane:
1. Select the appropriate role headline variant
2. Apply the section mapping guide to decide which evidence blocks to lead with
3. Use the ATS keyword insertion table to ensure the application passes automated screening
4. Use interview talking points to prepare the candidate for panel conversations
5. Check the "Do Not Claim" list before sending anything — items flagged there must not appear without candidate re-confirmation

## PART A: THREE ROLE LANE DEFINITIONS

---

### Lane A — Infrastructure Programme Director

**Target:** Roles titled Infrastructure Programme Director, Senior Infrastructure Delivery Director, Technical Programme Director, Head of Infrastructure Delivery  
**Typical employers:** MSPs, global consultancies, infrastructure integrators, enterprise IT departments, managed services providers  
**Day rate range:** £550–£750/day (contract)  
**What they want:** A senior delivery operator who can own a complex, multi-site infrastructure programme end-to-end, manage technical and commercial accountability simultaneously, and report confidently to client executives

**Profile emphasis:** Delivery leadership at scale · Multi-site scope signals · Technical breadth · Governance discipline · Programme mobilisation and recovery · Regulated environment credibility

**Headline to use:**
> *Infrastructure Programme Director | Network, Data Centre & EUC Transformation | Regulated UK/EMEA Estates*

**Lead evidence blocks (in this order):**
1. NATO 200+ airbase upgrade (scale, pan-European, on-time delivery)
2. CentricsIT 15+ concurrent EMEA programmes (scope and governance breadth)
3. LVS Bulgaria (greenfield complexity, multi-vendor, P&L accountability)
4. Lambeth 60% WAN cost reduction + £12M re-tender (commercial programme outcome)
5. Atos/NHS (current, mission-critical, zero-disruption)

**De-emphasise:** COO/MD framing; contract-to-cash; subscription model language; Ipitomi founder story (unless directly asked)

---

### Lane B — Senior Infrastructure Programme Manager

**Target:** Roles titled Senior Infrastructure PM, Senior Programme Manager (Infrastructure), IT Infrastructure Programme Manager, Technical Programme Manager  
**Typical employers:** Large enterprises, NHS/healthcare, local government, financial services, public sector integrators  
**Day rate range:** £450–£600/day (contract)  
**What they want:** A technically credible programme manager who can run a structured delivery lifecycle across a complex infrastructure estate, manage stakeholder relationships, and hold vendors to account

**Profile emphasis:** Structured delivery methodology · RAID and governance discipline · Stakeholder management · Technical breadth (LAN, WAN, EUC, DC) · GDS/public sector experience · Risk management

**Headline to use:**
> *Senior Infrastructure Programme Manager | Multi-Site Estate & Network Modernisation | Regulated Environments*

**Lead evidence blocks (in this order):**
1. Atos/NHS (current, zero-disruption, live estate — immediately relatable to most employers)
2. CentricsIT EMEA programmes (governance at scale)
3. Rainmaker — 7 Government departments, GDS delivery (public sector credibility)
4. Home Office M365 (42,000 users — EUC scale)
5. Lambeth 60% cost reduction + £12M re-tender (commercial programme management)

**De-emphasise:** PE investor language; COO framing; P&L ownership claims; Ipitomi; heavy commercial lifecycle language

### Lane C — Technical Operations Director (Interim)

**Target:** Roles titled Interim COO, Interim Technical Operations Director, Interim Head of Technology Operations, Contract Operations Director  
**Typical employers:** PE-backed technology and connectivity businesses, managed services providers, telecoms/connectivity scale-ups, high-growth tech firms  
**Day rate range:** £600–£750/day (contract); some inside IR35 depending on engagement model  
**What they want:** An operator who can hold the full technical operations lifecycle — service delivery, SLA governance, vendor management, billing integrity, commercial risk — in a business that is growing, transforming, or in need of stabilisation

**Profile emphasis:** P&L ownership · Contract-to-cash · Subscription and managed services operations · Vendor SLA governance · PE/investor interface · Board reporting · Operational efficiency/cost reduction · Delivery stabilisation

**Headline to use:**
> *Technical Operations Director (Interim) | Service Delivery, SLA Governance & Commercial Operations | PE-backed & High-Growth Environments*

**Lead evidence blocks (in this order):**
1. LVS Bulgaria (P&L, contract-to-cash, Board-level KPI packs, greenfield ops) — *confirm NDA status first*

## PART C: INTERVIEW TALKING POINTS FROM PROFILE

*These are structured from the evidence bank. Each has: the claim, the context sentence, and the expected follow-up question handled.*

### Talking Point 1: NATO Pan-European Network Upgrade
**Claim:** Led network infrastructure upgrade across 200+ NATO airbases across Europe — 30-person team, cross-Atlantic logistics, delivered on time.  
**Context:** "At CentricsIT I was acting as de-facto EMEA Operations Director across a portfolio of programmes. The NATO engagement was the highest-stakes — 200+ airbases, equipment moving from the US, American technical staff I was managing alongside European teams."  
**Pressure follow-up:** "When delivery started slipping, I didn't manage it from London. I flew to Germany, went to the source of the problem, restructured the on-ground team, and re-established the programme cadence. That pattern — go to where the problem is, don't manage it remotely — is how I operate under pressure."  
*Confidence note: Confirm full programme ownership vs stream lead before rehearsing this.*

---

### Talking Point 2: Lambeth 60% WAN Cost Reduction
**Claim:** Delivered 60% cost reduction across 200+ local authority sites; led £12M voice and data services re-tender.  
**Context:** "At Lambeth I was contracted initially on a compliance project, and then retained as Lead Enterprise Architect for 7 years. The 60% cost reduction wasn't handed to me — it was the result of redesigning the network architecture and running a rigorous competitive re-tender on a £12M voice and data contract."  
**Commercial follow-up:** "The re-tender gave me end-to-end commercial experience — defining the specification, managing the procurement process, evaluating supplier proposals, and governing performance post-award. That's the full lifecycle."

### Talking Point 3: Las Vegas Sands Bulgaria — Greenfield Operations
**Claim:** Country-level Operations Director for Las Vegas Sands' first European online gaming and HFT platform — ran P&L, inherited 24-person multilingual team, oversaw £3M low-latency network build.  
**Context:** "This was a greenfield mandate — nothing existed. I was brought in to define, build, and operate a revenue-generating infrastructure platform from the ground up. The complexity was multi-jurisdictional: European regulatory constraints, stakeholders in Asia and the US, a technology stack built for high-frequency trading latency requirements."  
**Board-level follow-up:** "I designed and chaired the governance framework myself — weekly service reviews through to Board-level KPI packs covering performance, risk, compliance, and commercial position. The Board could understand what the programme was doing because I translated it for them."  
*Confidence note: Confirm NDA status for LVS name before interview prep; have generic descriptor ready: "major international gaming group."*

## PART D: ATS KEYWORD INSERTION STRATEGY

### Rule: Lead with the job description's own language
Always mirror the specific terms used in the job advert. If the advert says "infrastructure transformation programme," use that exact phrase — not "technology change" or "digital transformation."

### Priority Insertion Points
1. **CV headline** — must contain the role's top 2–3 title keywords
2. **Profile statement, sentences 1–2** — insert primary domain keywords here (ATS reads top-first)
3. **Core skills / competencies block** — use the ATS keyword bank directly; match advert terms verbatim where possible
4. **Most recent roles** — repeat critical terms across first 3 roles (ATS weights recency)
5. **Earlier career section** — secondary keyword pass only
