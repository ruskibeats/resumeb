## Role Targeting Strategy

## A) Primary Market Lane (recommended)
**Infrastructure Programme Director / Senior Infrastructure Delivery Lead**

Why: strongest evidence density, broad UK demand, clean ATS alignment.

## B) Secondary Lane
**Technical Operations Director (interim)**

Why: credible for PE-backed and service-heavy environments; best where commercial lifecycle ownership matters.

## C) Situational Lane
**Assurance / recovery advisory roles**

Why: useful for high-risk change programmes, especially where discovery/reset capability is valued.

## UK Hiring Signals to Reflect in Applications

- “Mission-critical” / “business-critical” / “always-on” estate delivery
- “Regulated environments” and compliance-sensitive transformations
- Demonstrable scale signals (multi-site, multi-country, high endpoint/circuit counts)
- Governance maturity (RAID, steering reporting, dependency/risk control)
- Commercial discipline (vendor challenge, SLA outcomes, contract governance)
- Live-service migration capability (zero/low disruption, phased cutovers)

## Evidence Blocks Teammates Should Reuse in CV/Applications

1. **Large public/regulated programmes:** Home Office, NHS, local authority, government departments.
2. **Infrastructure modernisation breadth:** LAN/WAN/Wi-Fi, SD-WAN/SASE, data centre, EUC.
3. **Operational accountability:** service continuity, risk ownership, senior stakeholder assurance.
4. **Commercial governance:** vendor performance, delivery trade-offs, reporting to executive audiences.
5. **Security/trust signals:** SC + NPPV3 (where relevant to role).

## Red Flags to Avoid

- Over-positioning as purely strategic architect with weak delivery ownership language.
- Over-claiming COO scope for roles that are infra-delivery specific.
- Under-specifying technical keywords needed for ATS (e.g., SD-WAN/SASE, data centre migration, EUC).
