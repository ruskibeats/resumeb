## 5) Role Mandate
Technical Design Authority responsible for evaluating, designing, and costing bespoke and non-standard commercial network solutions that fell outside Virgin Media's standard product catalogue. Key programme: led (or was primary designer for) the Northern Ireland Schools broadband connectivity programme — connecting all 2,000 schools in Northern Ireland to the internet for the first time.

## 6) Core Responsibilities
- Served as Technical Design Authority for all non-standard and bespoke network solutions.

- Designed non-standard connectivity solutions (broadband, fibre, leased lines) where standard product catalogue did not apply.
- Managed early VoIP programmes and non-standard technical solutions.
- Delivered commercial and technical assessment for complex connectivity proposals.

**Northern Ireland Schools Broadband Programme:**
- Led (or was primary designer for) broadband, fibre, and leased-line connectivity design and delivery across all 2,000 schools in Northern Ireland, under a Department of Education (DoE) mandate.
- 2-year programme; 100% completion — first time every school in Northern Ireland had internet access.
- Mixed technology design required: each school assessed individually for appropriate connectivity (broadband, fibre, or leased line) — no standard solution applicable.

## 7) Senior Judgement Calls / Decisions

- **Per-school connectivity design** (NI programme) — determining which technology (broadband, fibre, leased line) was appropriate for each of 2,000 schools, based on location, infrastructure availability, and DoE requirements.
- **Commercial bid assessment** — evaluating the cost and technical risk of proposals including a 40,000+ node education network; Russell's sign-off shaped what could credibly be committed to in bids.
