## 3. STRUCTURAL & NARRATIVE ISSUES

### 3.1 The "Contractor Framing" Problem
**Severity: HIGH**

Every role from 2009 onwards is labelled "(Contractor)" or described as "Contract ...". For an MD/COO-level application, this can be read negatively:

- Recruiter heuristic: "contractors execute, permanent MDs own"
- Wireless Logic wants someone who "owns operational performance"

**Fix:** Remove "(Contractor)" tags from all role titles. Reframe as "embedded" or "engaged as" where necessary. The profile already positions it correctly — let the roles speak as leadership appointments.

### 3.2 Title Inflation Risk
**Severity: MEDIUM**

"Hiloka Ltd — Managing Director" is technically your own consultancy. Recruiters may discount this as self-employment rather than an external appointment. The Las Vegas Sands achievement is strong — lead with that, not the Hiloka entity.

**Fix:** Consider restructuring profile to list "Selected leadership engagements" rather than chronological employer history, or reposition Hiloka as the vehicle with client outcomes front-loaded.

### 3.3 Missing Education
**Severity: HIGH**

`[Education details to be added based on available information]` is an instant rejection risk at MD level. 32% of applicants for this role hold an MBA. Even without formal qualifications, this section must be populated.

**Fix:** Add whatever exists — qualifications, professional certifications (ITIL, Prince2, MSP, etc.), industry training, or replace with "Professional Development" section.

### 3.4 Experience Density — Older Roles
**Severity: LOW**

Roles pre-2009 (Comunica, Virgin Media, Charles Stanley, Whittington) are solid foundation stories but take significant space (12 bullets across 4 roles). For a 2-page UK CV, consider compressing early career to 2-3 lines max to free space for metrics and a stronger profile.

### 3.5 Overlapping Engagements Timeline
**Severity: MEDIUM**

Multiple roles marked "Present":
- Hiloka: Jan 2010 – Present
- Collective IP: Jun 2025 – Present  
- Solutions Through Knowledge: Jan 2023 – Present

Overlap is legitimate for consultancy, but needs a one-liner explanation ("Concurrent advisory and embedded leadership engagements") or the chronological view will confuse ATS and human readers.
